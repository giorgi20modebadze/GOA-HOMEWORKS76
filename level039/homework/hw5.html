1. Web developer

Primary duties: Web developers design and create websites. They're responsible for both how the site looks and how it functions. They test and evaluate a site to make sure it meets quality standards before its release. After the site gets published, web developers monitor the site's continued performance and troubleshoot any technical problems that might arise. They ensure that their websites and programs meet current security standards. On an average job, a web developer first meets with a client to discuss their vision for a project. Then they write code for the site and work with a team of other creators to produce content for the website. Requirements to pursue: Most employers require their web developers to hold a bachelor's degree in computer science or a similar field.





2. computer programmer

Primary duties: Computer programmers write and edit code for computer software programs and ensure the programs run with no errors. Computer programmers solve any issues or problems that arise during the coding process and make sure that the final product meets their clients' standards. They might also be responsible for training others to use their programs once they complete the software. Requirements to pursue: Some requirements for becoming a computer programmer include earning a bachelor's degree in computer science or computer programming. Learning to read and write in a variety of coding languages is also useful.





3. Computer systems engineer

Primary duties: Computer systems engineers work to make sure their clients' computer systems are all fully functional and efficient. They can work directly for an organization to maintain and improve their systems or for a third-party company that provides these services for multiple clients. Computer systems engineers are also responsible for the maintenance and security of a company's systems, including protecting the company's privacy and data. Computer systems engineers also innovate ideas to improve the functionality of their employers' systems. They advise and provide updated information to the company's informational technology departments. They may also advise users directly to help them understand how to use systems efficiently.





4. Systems analyst

Primary duties: A systems analyst evaluates a business' technology and looks for ways to fix issues or optimize systems. Also called a system designer or system architect, a systems analyst specializes in offering businesses recommendations on how to create personalized IT systems that meet their needs. Specific responsibilities may include researching the latest organizational technologies, performing cost-benefit analysis to determine the best system upgrades and training end users on how to use the new technology at the company where they work. Requirements to pursue: Prospective employers typically prefer that systems analysts hold at least a bachelor's degree in a subject like computer science or information technology. A systems analyst is often a more advanced position at a company, so individuals interested in pursuing this field might first gain experience in entry-level technology positions.





5. Programmer analyst

Primary duties: A career as a programmer analyst combines the jobs of a systems analyst and a computer programmer. Programmer analysts design and create computer software and programs. Working with other computer programmers, a programmer analyst then oversees the writing of the code necessary to create and publish the programs. Employers hire programmer analysts to create specific programs that fit the individual needs of the organization. Programmer analysts update existing programs and systems so they're compatible with the latest technologies. They may also write or edit user support documentation that advises their client or employer on how to use the programs. Requirements to pursue: Common requirements for programming analysts are a bachelor's degree in computer science, proficiency in coding languages and several years of application development experience.
